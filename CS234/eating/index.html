Our Food Project

A Dietary Experiment

  • CS: 234

    • Summary

    For one week, students collected data about their daily eating habits as honestly as possible. Data was collected in different ways, cleaned, and then analyzed to find patterns about Wellesley College Students.

    Introduction

    I conducted this experiment on the Wellesley College campus in a multitude of locations depending on where I ate. I expected to be disappointed with my eating habits as I already know they are not the best. One of the biggest challenges I had with this experiment was remembering to collect data before and after every meal.

    Data Collection

    My method of data collection was taking pictures before and after each meal or snack depending on if I remembered to do so. I was able to collect data for 21 meals and snacks.

    Findings

    While analying my data I was able to study patterns of my diet. Throughout my data I was pleasantly surprised to learn that I drank water at every meal. On the other hand I realized that I should stop replacing meals for sugarey snacks while I'm on the go. My lack of time to spend on eating is also apparent as most of my breakfasts are in the form of handheld granola bars and easily portable food to eat while walking.

    Conclusion

    Through the collection of food data, new information relating to the life and wellbeing of students can be developed. In the high-pressure atmosphere of Wellesey it's easy to let school work come before personal health. Thus the diets of our students can show more about levels of stress than students would like to admit. The quality and preference of dining hall food should also be noted. If students are throwng away large portions of food it could be related to the way the food tasted and its quality, also commenting on the food provided in college dining halls across the country.
